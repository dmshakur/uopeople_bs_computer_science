Part 1

There are two parts of this equation that need to be considered when finding the domain. The first is that we cannot get the square root of a number less than 0, and the second is we cannot have the denominator of a fraction to be equal to 0. Both square root operations in this equation are being subtracted from, so then x must be a value that is either equal to or greater then the largest number we are getting the difference from, out of both negatives 6 is the largest, so x ≤ 6. Since the second square root is getting the difference of x and 4 we don’t need to consider its impact since, x ≤ 6, and any number that conforms to that specification will not create a number less than or equal to 0. Therefore the domain of $f(x)=\frac{\sqrt{x-6}}{\sqrt{x-4}}$ is [6, ∞).
